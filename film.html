The Last Date

  • Based on a true story, "The Last Date" is a thriller that follows Leah as she discovers that her first date after a recent breakup is not what she expected.

  • Directed and Edited by Adithya Rajan
  • Written by Adithya Rajan and Sachit Grover
  • Cast: Audrey Marie Craddick, Sachit Grover, and Ella Calleri

  • Official Selection: Golden Cali Film and Art Awards

Knock / Knock

  • I woke up the morning of halloween and suddenly felt like I needed to put out a film in time for the holiday. That gave me a little more than 12 hours to conceptualize, write, shoot, and edit an entire short film! Definitely a daunting task but I was determined. Unfortunately (As you can see from the date that this video was posted) I didn’t finish in time for halloween. But hey, finishing in under 36 hours isn’t too bad! The film is a little rough around the edges given the time constraints I had placed on myself but I hope you enjoy it!
  • I definitely learned a lot while making this film since I again made it entirely alone and also since I had placed certain time restrictions on myself. I had to learn how to make my process much more efficient and sometimes sacrifice smaller details to help enable a better overall product. I've definitely felt myself improve as a filmmaker since my first film and am excited to make more!

  • Written, Directed, and Edited by Adithya Rajan

Imposter Syndrome

  • Imposter Syndrome: "Anxiety or self-doubt that results from persistently undervaluing one’s competence and active role in achieving success, while falsely attributing one's accomplishments to luck or other external forces"
  • I sometimes experience this myself when I'm creating or even in other parts of my life like my education and my work. The other day I was finding it extremely difficult to find motivation while writing and suddenly I had the idea to channel those feelings into a new project. This is that project, and maybe you can relate to these feelings. This may be the most "honest" script that I've written in terms of looking into myself and understanding my own strengths and faults. I think many of us may have our own "impostors" and it's important to acknowledge our own talent and hard work in order to overcome them.

  • Written, Directed, and Edited by Adithya Rajan
